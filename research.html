Major studies & collaborations advancing sustainable resource recovery.

Feasibility Study to Implement Hydrothermal Liquefaction in Southeast Michigan

Funding: U.S. Department of Energy

This collaboration between SWR2L, Great Lakes Water Authority, PNNL, Brown & Caldwell, Genifuel, U-M, and Wayne State measures sustainability of HTL for sewage sludge via LCA, techno-economic analysis, and community engagement.

Treatment & Resource Recovery from HTL Aqueous By-product

Funding: U.S. Department of Energy , Water Research Foundation, GLWA

We use anaerobic digestion and chemical processes to recover biogas & ammonia from HTL aqueous effluent, simulating activated sludge processes at WRRFs.

PFAS Fate During Hydrothermal Liquefaction of Sewage Sludge

Funding: U.S. Department of Energy

Evaluating transformation and fate of PFAS in continuous HTL reactor effluents (biocrude, gas, liquid, solid) using Genifuel pilot unit.

Nitrification Inhibition by Propylene Glycol (Austin WRRF)

Funding: City of Austin (AW)

Bench-scale bioreactors simulate secondary treatment to study inhibition of nitrifiers by propylene glycol.

Unleashing Anaerobic Fungi in Anaerobic Digestion

Funding: University of Texas at Austin

Developing rumen-like bioprocesses using anaerobic fungi (Neocallimastigomycota) to convert lignocellulose to biofuels without costly pretreatment.
